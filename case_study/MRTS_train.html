MRTS User Journey Observation

    UX Researcher

  • Project Duration

    2 weeks

  • Team size

    Individual project

Overview

MRTS train journey observation was conducted in Chennai, from Velacherry MRTS station to Park Town. The aim was to basically observe the infrastructure, nuances of the journey, hidden complexity of passengers while transit and suggestions for enhancement.

 

Criticism

Inadequate maintenance and usage of station facilities. Improper maintenance of stations, vandalism. non-functional station amenities such as lifts and escalators, passengers insecurity.

 

My Role

I captured the social and behavioural pattern of the passengers, demography, transit model, design as well as amenities of the train and the problem behind.

 

Journey in State of Paradox

  • MRTS Station, having enormous space inside and outside face the contradictory of being largely deserted at times.
  • People rarely use the station entrance and flee to board the train through a short cut in the broken side walls of the entrance.
  • Stairs need to be used to reach the platform from entrance, whereas the shortcut leads directly to the platform slope
  • Every station has paid restrooms, elevators, lifts but all remains locked or closed with no reasons
  • Ticket vending machine hardly serves the purpose, so there is an undeniable long queue at ticket counter.

Design with clarity

For every stop and start the train honks to alert the passengers to board or get down.

General compartments, first class and ladies compartments are classified accurately with words and pictorial representation. MRTS uses the same gauge as the suburban system, thus allowing the movements of passengers between the existing suburban lines and the MRTS. Do not have automatic doors like a metro train.

 

Persona

Passengers who commute to work are the avid users of MRTS Train. In a highly populated country like India, train is considered to be a common mode of transporation for all genders across ages. And I could identify 4 key personas emerging out of the observation.

Suggestions for improvement

Rent out the station space for commercial purpose to attract more crowd as well as get more revenues

Ticket vending machines should be in a running condition. Special focus would be on pedestrian facilities that would provide access to MRTS stations from nearby roads and bus stops, apart from identifying new feeder routes.

Integrated ticketing systems, better facilities for passengers, increased frequency of services taking into consideration of passenger demands, promise public safety and also better maintenance.

 

Learning

Integration with chennai metro would not bring better connectivity inside the city but also bring more usage rate. Design and Experience can influence the revenue model of a state or country, shape the perception, influence the behavior and eventually build a better future.
