Academy Sports

B2E Mobile App for Intrabusiness Network

  • My Role

    UX/UI Designer

  • Project Duration

    1 month

  • Team size

    1 Designer and 1 Business Analyst

Overview

Academy Sports + Outdoors philosophy is to deliver an unparalleled shopping experience by providing convenience, offering a broad selection of quality products, delivering exceptional customer service and selling the right stuff at every day low prices.

 

My Role

I was chosen to tackle both experience and visual design of the mobile application. I wanted the visual design to mock recent trends while still keeping a streamlined user experience that supported the mobile app.

Challenge

Academy Sports is one of the US nation's largest sporting goods and outdoor stores, accomplishing exactly what they set out to do and they have scaled up business to employees.On the product level, there is rarely any complaints.

Users search and wait–in anxiousness– to delve into & shop what is new in discount sales in a premier sports wear, outdoor lifestyle and much more.. This is a repetitive process that can be made more efficient for any user, so I set out to design a concept feature to do it.

 

Approach

To gain a deeper understanding I talked to 4 Academy Sports users over phone. Here are the key highlights from these chats:

  • The majority of users are young adults, bread-winners of the family
  • There are 5 main occasions users would like to order products: high-quality hunting, fishing, camping equipment and gear along with sports and leisure products
  • Majority order products regularly for sports footwear, apparel almost for all members of the family
  • They are quick to try other online- sale offers if there are no academy+sports sale at that moment
  • The range of Shipping discount applied at checkout is also another factor users consider (promotional offer in shipping rate)
  • Speed and convenience in exchange or refund of the merchandise.

To sum up my findings, I made a quick persona. Throughout the design process, I refered back to this persona to prioritize and inform my design decisions.

User Stories

To keep in mind the main problems users face, and why they would need such a feature, I created some user stories.

Here are some examples of the Epics ("big" user stories):

  • As a regular user, I want to collapse my purchase order anytime based on the total cost.
  • As a regular user, I want to set a restriction on the surge amount so that I am comfortable and prepared to pay the market price.
  • User stories can vary in granularity and be extremely detailed as well.

Wireframe

I wanted to create the features with as little hassle to the user as possible.

With a decision made, I laid out hi-fidelity wireframes with detailed descriptions of how the elements will behave.

This was actually used internally to help support a script/narrative that directed what we built and for a demo that the presenter only saw given that this part of the product is already built. So as we went through the script, we also had visual as a reference.

The Solution

We believe our users are intelligent, creative folk who would enjoy being inspired by all means. So we created a series of screens based on user phenomena that are not stand alone - indeed blends with user interaction

Success Stories

Having to work with remote teams across locations, I was clearly aware of the misinterpretations that are expected to happen. Deliberately, I entrusted myself to transcript discussions, document my critical findings through user research, task flow, information architecture, wireframe sketches to communicate and crosscheck team's understanding in every standpoint.
